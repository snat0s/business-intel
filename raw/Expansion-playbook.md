### **Agent Pekka Global Expansion Playbook**

#### **1\. Analyzing and Prioritizing Locations**

* **Creating a Longlist and Shortlist**:  
  * Conduct detailed analysis of potential locations, focusing on market potential, client presence, and cultural fit.  
  * Rank locations to generate a longlist, then refine it into a shortlist of priority areas for expansion.  
* **Simultaneous Searches**:  
  * Initiate searches for founding agents in 5-10 shortlisted locations simultaneously to optimize time and resources.

#### **2\. Finding and Selecting the Founding Agent**

* **Role Importance**: The founding agent acts as the cornerstone of the new branch, often starting as the sole representative in the location.  
* **Candidate Criteria**:  
  * Deep connections within the local creative and illustration community.  
  * Strong background in art, business development, or related industries.  
  * Exceptional organizational and communication skills.  
* **Assessment and Evaluation**:  
  * Perform thorough background checks and reference verifications.  
  * Ensure alignment with Agent Pekka’s values and ability to represent the brand effectively.  
* **Outreach Strategy**:  
  * Leverage existing artist networks for recommendations.  
  * Use LinkedIn and other professional platforms to find and approach potential candidates.  
* **Process Trigger**:  
  * Selecting a founding agent marks the start of the office setup process in the chosen location.

#### **3\. Setting Up New Offices**

* **Legal and Administrative Framework**:  
  * Establish a local company fully owned by Agent Pekka Oy, with the founding agent holding a 15% ownership stake.  
  * Ensure compliance with local legal requirements for entity formation, including drafting necessary corporate documentation.  
  * Secure a registered address through leasing or shared office services.  
  * Hire a bookkeeper to ensure financial records comply with local standards.  
* **Flexible Operational Models**:  
  * **Freelance through Founding Agent’s Company**:  
    * Allow the agent to freelance for Agent Pekka Oy via their own company for 12 months. This provides a cost-effective way to test the market.  
    * Transition to a formal structure if the market responds positively.  
  * **Establish a Local Subsidiary**:  
    * Set up a local entity within 2-3 months.  
    * Have the founding agent freelance through their company for the first 12 months before transitioning to direct operations.  
  * **Direct Employment**:  
    * Hire the founding agent as a salaried employee under the newly formed entity, though this is the least flexible and most costly option.  
* **Physical Setup**:  
  * Operate from shared offices or co-working spaces to minimize costs while ensuring access to essential facilities.  
  * Equip the founding agent with necessary tools, including a laptop, phone, and reliable internet connection.

#### **4\. Marketing, Advertising, and PR Strategy**

* **Market Research**:  
  * Identify strategies and platforms to engage target audiences effectively.  
  * Analyze local media, events, and cultural touchpoints.  
* **Launch Campaigns**:  
  * Organize impactful events or campaigns to announce the new branch.  
  * Collaborate with local influencers and creative organizations to amplify visibility.  
* **Supporting Materials**:  
  * Provide the founding agent with branded marketing materials tailored to the new market.  
  * Ensure uniformity in messaging across all locations.  
* **Sustained Marketing**:  
  * Plan ongoing advertising initiatives, including digital, print, and event-based promotions.

#### **5\. Training and Development for Founding Agents**

* **Comprehensive Onboarding**:  
  * Deliver in-depth training on Agent Pekka’s values, tone of voice, and operations.  
  * Facilitate workshops on client management, artist collaboration, and business strategies.  
  * Transition existing clients in the area to the founding agent with guidance from an experienced agent.  
* **Ongoing Development**:  
  * Offer continuous access to professional development resources.  
  * Schedule regular check-ins to monitor progress and provide support.

#### **6\. Operational Tools and Processes**

* **CRM and Project Management**:  
  * Provide access to centralized systems for tracking clients and managing projects.  
* **Communication Infrastructure**:  
  * Set up official email accounts and secure communication channels.  
  * Establish routine reporting to maintain transparency and alignment with headquarters.

#### **7\. Introducing Artists to New Markets**

* **Strategic Integration**:  
  * Host launch events and exhibitions to showcase artists’ work.  
  * Partner with local creative hubs to build community engagement.  
  * Use digital platforms and social media to expand reach.

#### **8\. Maintaining Brand Tone and Identity**

* **Consistent Messaging**:  
  * Reflect Agent Pekka’s established tone—approachable, professional, and creative.  
  * Highlight the unique talents and stories of represented artists.  
  * Build trust with clients by emphasizing collaboration.

#### **9\. Adapting to Local Culture**

* **Cultural Sensitivity**:  
  * Train teams to understand and respect local customs and communication styles.  
  * Customize marketing and outreach strategies to align with regional norms while maintaining brand integrity.

#### **10\. Tracking Milestones and Goals**

* **First 3 Months**:  
  * Finalize office setup and establish foundational operations.  
  * Begin networking with local clients and communities.  
* **6-12 Months**:  
  * Secure major client projects.  
  * Host an event or campaign to solidify market presence.  
  * Aim for financial breakeven by the end of the first year.

#### **11\. Ongoing Support and Oversight**

* **Regular Communication**:  
  * Maintain open lines between the new office and headquarters.  
  * Conduct feedback sessions to address challenges and share insights.  
* **Site Visits**:  
  * Schedule periodic visits to strengthen relationships and evaluate progress.

To find the right candidates in volume for leading your new operational locations, you’ll need a systematic and multi-pronged recruitment strategy tailored to your specific needs. Here’s a step-by-step guide:  
---

### **1\. Define the Ideal Candidate Profile**

Refine the detailed profile of the person you’re looking for. Based on your earlier hires, include:

* **Background**: Experience in agency work, as an art buyer, or similar roles.  
* **Skills**: Strong network in the creative industry, ability to interact with and manage artists, project management, entrepreneurial mindset.  
* **Traits**: Well-liked, proactive, adaptable, and driven by results.  
* **Incentives**: Comfortable with a low base salary and performance-based compensation.

Use this profile to create tailored job descriptions and screening criteria.

---

### **2\. Identify Key Channels to Source Talent**

Leverage platforms and networks relevant to the creative and advertising industries to cast a wide net:

* **Creative Industry Networks**: Post in specialized job boards like Behance, Working Not Working, and Arts Thread.  
* **Professional Networks**: Use LinkedIn for targeted outreach, particularly to people in art buying, production, and agency roles.  
* **Creative Communities**: Engage with professional organizations (e.g., AIGA, ADC Global, or D\&AD).  
* **Social Media**: Use Instagram and Twitter to connect with art buyers, producers, and creative freelancers who might fit the entrepreneurial mindset.  
* **Referrals**: Tap into your existing network, artists, and clients to recommend potential candidates.

---

### **3\. Build a Targeted Recruitment Campaign**

Focus your messaging to attract entrepreneurial-minded individuals who resonate with your vision:

* **Highlight Unique Opportunities**:  
  * Ability to work with top-tier artists.  
  * Autonomy to shape the local market.  
  * Performance-driven rewards.  
* **Emphasize Growth Potential**:  
  * Potential to grow the location into a major hub.  
  * The chance to be part of a global expansion journey.  
* **Create a Compelling Employer Brand**:  
  * Showcase Agent Pekka's innovative approach, success stories, and the quality of its artists.

Invest in professional videos, case studies, and testimonials to attract interest.

---

### **4\. Deploy a Systematic Screening Process**

Given the importance of finding the right candidates, create a robust recruitment funnel:

* **Step 1: Pre-screening**:  
  * Use online application forms to assess experience, networks, and motivations.  
* **Step 2: Screening Calls**:  
  * Test for entrepreneurial mindset and ability to work on performance-based pay.  
* **Step 3: In-depth Interviews**:  
  * Explore their ability to build networks, lead artist relationships, and grow a business.  
* **Step 4: Cultural Fit**:  
  * Assess how well they align with Agent Pekka’s ethos through creative challenges or assignments.

---

### **5\. Consider Partnering with Recruitment Specialists**

Work with recruiters who specialize in the creative or advertising industries, particularly those with global reach. Examples include:

* **Global Recruitment Agencies**: Aquent, The Creative Group.  
* **Boutique Agencies**: Agencies specializing in creative leadership hires.

These agencies can leverage their networks and expertise to bring in high-quality candidates quickly.

---

### **6\. Leverage Local Market Knowledge**

* Engage local creatives or consultants to help identify potential hires in each target city.  
* Collaborate with local design schools, advertising networks, or artist communities for introductions to potential candidates.

---

### **7\. Use Events to Network and Source Candidates**

Participate in or host events targeting creatives in your priority cities:

* Creative industry conferences and expos.  
* Local art shows and design festivals.  
* Agent Pekka’s branded networking or portfolio review events.

Use these events as platforms to scout and connect with potential candidates.

---

### **8\. Consider a "Test Before Hire" Model**

To ensure candidates are the right fit, you could:

* Hire them on a probationary or contract basis.  
* Offer freelance or part-time roles before transitioning to full leadership.

---

### **9\. Adjust the City Priority List Dynamically**

If you find excellent candidates in a city that wasn’t high on your priority list, consider shifting the focus to capitalize on the talent.

---

### **10\. Monitor and Optimize**

Track the effectiveness of each channel and adapt your strategy:

* Which channels are yielding the best candidates?  
* What feedback are you getting from candidates on the role and expectations?  
* Are you closing hires efficiently?

Use this data to refine your approach as you expand into the next cities.
