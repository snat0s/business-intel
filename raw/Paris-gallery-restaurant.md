### Operational Structure

The project represents a strategic partnership with clearly defined responsibilities:

**Agency Responsibilities:**
- All costs related to the art gallery: display, transportation, insurance
- Branding, website, and overall marketing
- A portion of the rent reflecting the gallery's space
- Costs related to the agency's office and client entertainment

**Restaurant Responsibilities:**
- All restaurant operational costs: food, beverages, kitchen equipment, staff
- The remaining portion of the rent reflecting the dining area
- Restaurant-specific insurance and licenses

**Shared Responsibilities:**
- Marketing will be done both separately and in joint efforts
- Rent will be split according to a pre-agreed percentage
- Joint events will be collaborative efforts

**Financial Separation:**
- Restaurant profits remain within the restaurant operation
- Agency profits remain within the agency
- This maintains complete financial clarity while leveraging synergies

## Comparable Concepts & Case Studies

### Restaurant-Gallery Hybrids

1. **Sketch (London)**
   - Features rotating art exhibitions throughout its various dining spaces
   - Particularly known for commissioning major artists for large-scale installations
   - Multiple dining concepts under one roof, from casual to Michelin-starred

2. **L'Avenue at Saks (New York)**
   - Luxury department store Saks Fifth Avenue partnered with Parisian restaurant L'Avenue
   - Features rotating art installations and designer elements
   - Creates a cultural retail-dining experience

3. **Colette (Paris, closed 2017)**
   - While now closed, this concept store with restaurant was a pioneer in combining retail, art, and food
   - Hosted rotating exhibitions and cultural events
   - Demonstrated viability of the multi-concept cultural space in Paris

4. **Chez Nina at Maximilian Harden (Berlin)**
   - Members-only art-focused dining club above K√∂nig Galerie
   - Invitation-only dining concept paired with contemporary art
   - Creates exclusivity while building cultural community

5. **The Modern at MoMA (New York)**
   - Fine dining restaurant integrated with the Museum of Modern Art
   - Benefits from institutional support while maintaining separate operations
   - Clear example of cultural institution partnering with dining experience

### Nordic-Influenced Cultural Venues

1. **The Marshall House (Reykjavik)**
   - Mixed-use cultural building with galleries, studios, and restaurant
   - Represents Nordic aesthetic in a historic industrial building
   - Restaurant La Primavera operates independently within the cultural space

2. **Fotografiska (Stockholm, New York, Tallinn)**
   - Photography museum with high-end restaurant concepts
   - Stockholm location features restaurant led by renowned Nordic chef Paul Svensson
   - Demonstrates successful scaling of cultural dining concept internationally

3. **Lyle's and Flor (London)**
   - While not galleries, these restaurants by James Lowe feature Nordic-influenced cuisine and feature rotating art
   - Minimalist Scandinavian aesthetic with strong creative programming

### Lessons from Similar Ventures

1. **Clear Identity Division**
   - Most successful hybrid concepts maintain distinct brand identities while creating synergy
   - Visitors understand what each part of the experience offers

2. **Programming Rhythm**
   - Successful spaces maintain consistent exhibition schedules and event calendars
   - Creates repeat visits and builds loyalty across both restaurant and gallery audiences

3. **Complementary Aesthetics**
   - Design language remains consistent between dining and exhibition spaces
   - Creates cohesive experience despite functional differences

4. **Revenue Stream Balance**
   - Most sustainable models don't rely exclusively on either dining or cultural components
   - Special events and private functions often become critical revenue drivers

5. **Cultural Authenticity**
   - The most respected venues maintain curatorial integrity despite commercial pressures
   - Avoids feeling like themed dining or superficial art integration
