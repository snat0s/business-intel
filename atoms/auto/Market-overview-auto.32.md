* **Diverse Client Relationships & Big-Name Projects:** Leading agencies cultivate relationships across **key client segments** – advertising agencies, brands (tech, fashion, FMCG, etc.), publishers, and entertainment companies. Having a roster of **blue-chip clients** serves as social proof and fuels a cycle of success. Competitors like B\&A and Handsome Frank showcase client lists featuring Apple, Nike, Amazon, Coca-Cola, and more ([Gitzwart | Bernstein & Andriulli | Great Bowery](https://www.gitzwart.com/work/bernstein-andriulli-great-bowery/#:~:text=Clients%20include%3A%20Adobe%2C%20Apple%2C%20Canada,BBDO%2C%20Droga5%2C%20TBWA%2FMedia%20Arts%20Lab)) ([About • Handsome Frank • Home to the world's greatest illustrators](https://www.handsomefrank.com/about#:~:text=Clients)), which boosts their credibility to win new business. Successful agencies often have **repeat clients** and referrals from major campaigns. Importantly, landing projects with high visibility (e.g., a nationwide ad campaign, a cover of a famous magazine, a viral social media campaign) propels an agency’s and its artists’ fame. The ability to navigate and maintain relationships with large clients – understanding their brand needs, procurement processes, and timelines – is a crucial factor. **Global reach** is also key: agencies that can service clients internationally (via multiple offices or simply experience shipping art worldwide) have an advantage as brands operate globally.
