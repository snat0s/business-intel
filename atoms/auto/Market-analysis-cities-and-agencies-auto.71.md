**9\. Toronto (Canada):** *Canada’s Commercial and Media Center.* Toronto is the largest city in Canada and the heart of its advertising and media industry. Many U.S. and international campaigns extend to Toronto for the Canadian market, and the city’s own ad ([Top 15 Artist-Friendly Cities: A Creative Haven for Your Next Chapter](https://www.artmiabo.com/2024/07/top-15-artist-friendly-cities-creative.html#:~:text=Chapter%20www,Chiang%20Mai%2C%20Thailand%3A%20%C2%B7%204)) encies (like those serving the banking, telecom, and retail sectors) commission illustrations. Toronto also has a sizable publishing industry (including Canadian divisions of major book publishers and numerous magazines). **Industries:** Advertising (consumer brands, tourism, retail), publishing (there’s a strong market for children’s books and educational publishing in Canada), and **corporate communications** (illustrations for annual reports, infographics for government or corporate outreach are common). *Notable agencies:* While Canada doesn’t have as many large illustration agencies as the US/UK, it does have **Anna Goodson Illustration Agency** in Montreal which serves all of Canada (and globally) – many Toronto clients work with AGIA for talent. Toronto also has agencies like **Illustration Online** (formerly “Three in a Box”) which historically was a big collective of Canadian illustrators. **House of Illustration** (Toronto) and **Shard Illustration** are smaller reps there. Additionally, global agencies often handle Canadian projects out of New York or London, but the volume of work centered in Toronto is high enough to rank it in the global top 10\. The city’s illustrators often straddle the U.S. market as well, given geographic proximity.
